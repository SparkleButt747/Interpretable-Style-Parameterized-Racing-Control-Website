# MPCC — Weights & Constraints

## What the weights do

> Style emerges **implicitly** from weights. Useful, but not one-knob-one-effect.

- $w_c$: hug path (lateral adherence)  
- $w_\ell$: don’t “lag” (keep up along track)  
- $w_p$: push progress  
- $w_u$: penalise big inputs  
- $w_r$: penalise input **rates** (smoothness)

## Constraints you’ll feel

- **Dynamics** (kinematic bicycle, discretised)  
- **Actuators:** $|\delta|, |\Delta\delta|, |a_x|$ bounded  
- **Friction:** $(a_x)^2 + (v^2\kappa)^2 \le (\mu g)^2$  
- **Track limits** (optional soft/hard bounds)

## A minimal tuning recipe

1) Start neutral (mid $w_c,w_\ell$; low $w_u,w_r$; mid $w_p$)  
2) Raise $w_c$ to fix adherence (watch for chatter)  
3) Increase $w_p$ until apex slip appears → back off 10–20%  
4) Lift $w_r$ (then a touch of $w_u$) to tame jerk  
5) Rebalance $w_\ell$ for straights vs corners

## Failure signatures (recognise them fast)

- **Over-contouring:** oscillatory steering; saw-tooth $\Delta\delta$; progress stalls  
- **Over-progress:** missed-apex flags ↑; friction donut saturates; lat-g spikes mid-corner  
- **Over-smooth:** pretty inputs; late apex; lap-time loss
