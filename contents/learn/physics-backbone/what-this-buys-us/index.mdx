## What this model captures
- Geometry & timing of path following (steer + speed)
- Curvature → feasible speed (friction-limited)
- Preview effects (lookahead trade-offs)
- Simple command shaping (rate/jerk)
- Style knobs → predictable behaviour

## What it does not capture
- Tyre minutiae (Pacejka, relaxation, temperature, wear)
- Load transfer, chassis roll/pitch, aero downforce
- Powertrain & shifting, regen blending
- Actuator dynamics (steer/brake lag, hysteresis)
- Perception stack (vision/SLAM)
- Track features (banking, kerbs, bumps, wet patches)

<Separator className="my-10" />

## Where it breaks (expect this)
- Snap S-bends at high μ look cleaner than reality
- Trail-braking nuances are approximated
- High-downforce corners are misrepresented (no aero)
- Kerbs/roughness ignored; trajectories don’t react

## Why this is still the right tool
- We’re teaching **control ideas** and **style interpretability**
- Fast iterations, stable sims, clear cause → effect
- Sliders produce **predictable** visual/metric deltas

<Separator className="my-10" />

## Sandboxing realism (guarantees)
- Deterministic replays (fixed seeds)
- Bounded commands \(|\delta|, |\dot\delta|, |a_x|\); jerk limited
- Conservative, smoothed \(v_t(s)\) to avoid spikes

<Separator className="my-10" />

## Calibration notes
- Check \(a_y = v^2 |\kappa|\) ≤ \(\mu g\)
- Track **missed-apex** / **overshoot** counts
- Compare to **MPCC-like ghost** on micro tracks

## Plain-English disclaimer
This simulator is a **clarity machine**. It’s great for learning preview, curvature-aware speed, and style trade-offs.  
It is **not** a tyre model, a setup tool, or a guarantee of real-car performance.
