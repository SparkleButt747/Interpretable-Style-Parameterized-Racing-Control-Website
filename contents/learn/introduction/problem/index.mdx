## The control interface

**State (assumed known)**: $(x,y,\psi,v)$  
**Path**: $\gamma(s)$ with curvature $\kappa(s)$  
**Outputs**: $\delta$ and $a_x$

$$
\pi:\{x,y,\psi,v,\gamma,\kappa\}\rightarrow\{\delta,a_x\}.
$$

## Naive baseline (useful because it fails)

**Idea:** aim toward the next checkpoint.

$$
\delta = \text{clip}(k_\delta \cdot e_\psi,\;-\delta_{\max},\;\delta_{\max})
$$

Throttle rule (example):
$$
a_x = a_{\max}\left(1-\left|\frac{e_\psi}{e_{\psi,\max}}\right|\right)
$$

## Typical failure modes

- Small $L$ → oscillation / chatter  
- Large $L$ → lazy turning / wide apex  
- Ignores that tighter curvature demands lower speed  
- Commands can be rough without rate limiting
