## State and commands

**State**
- Position and heading $(x,y,\psi)$
- Speed $v$

**Commands**
- Steering $\delta$
- Longitudinal acceleration $a_x$

$$
\pi:\{x,y,\psi,v,\gamma,\kappa,\mu\}\rightarrow\{\delta,a_x\}.
$$

## Kinematic bicycle (teaching model)

$$
\dot{x}=v\cos(\psi+\beta),\quad
\dot{y}=v\sin(\psi+\beta),\quad
\dot{\psi}=\frac{v}{L}\sin\beta,\quad
\beta=\arctan\!\Big(\frac{l_r}{L}\tan\delta\Big),\quad
\dot{v}=a_x.
$$

We simulate at 60–120 Hz (Euler/RK2 is sufficient for stable demos).

## Friction limit (combined demand)

We limit combined lateral and longitudinal acceleration:

$$
(a_x)^2 + (v^2\kappa)^2 \le (\mu g)^2.
$$

Interpretation: at higher curvature $|\kappa|$, allowable speed shrinks.

## Curvature → target speed

A simple target speed rule:

$$
v_t(s)\approx \sqrt{\frac{\mu g}{|\kappa(s)|+\epsilon}}.
$$

Then we smooth $v_t(s)$ (so it is teachable and doesn’t spike).

## Actuator bounds and rate limits

We enforce:

$$
|\delta|\le \delta_{\max},\quad |\dot{\delta}|\le \dot{\delta}_{\max},\quad |a_x|\le a_{x,\max}.
$$

We also optionally limit jerk:

$$
| \dot{a}_x | \le j_{\max}.
$$
